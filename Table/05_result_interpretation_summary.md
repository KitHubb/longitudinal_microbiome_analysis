# Result interpretation summary

- Alpha diversity: Shannon diversity increased with time_months (estimate = 0.0158, p = 0.004246). Group effect and time × group interaction were not significant (group p = 0.7883; interaction p = 0.8301).
  Interpretation: Alpha diversity appears to increase over follow-up time, but there is no evidence that this trajectory differs between T1D and control groups.

- Beta diversity: Bray-Curtis distance from baseline increased with time_from_baseline_months (estimate = 0.0219, p = 9.832e-12). Group effect and time × group interaction were not significant (group p = 0.7326; interaction p = 0.7753).
  Interpretation: Within-subject community composition progressively diverges from baseline over time, but the degree of divergence is not clearly different between T1D and control groups.

- Subject-level beta diversity: Subject-level PERMANOVA did not support a group-level community difference (R2 = 0.0270, p = 0.986).
  Interpretation: After reducing pseudoreplication by averaging samples at the subject level, there is no clear overall group separation.

- Taxa-level: Taxa-level CLR-LMM found 3 FDR-significant time-associated taxa and 0 FDR-significant time × group interaction taxa.
  Interpretation: Taxa-level interpretation should focus on FDR-significant time effects. Nominal interaction signals should be treated as exploratory only.
